# Robinson-Huron Treaty member explainer

Reference URL: https://oiatc.ca/explainers/robinson-huron-treaty

## Facebook — Russell Jones personal (manual)

Being honest with you: I do not fully understand what is happening with the Robinson-Huron Treaty annuities right now, and I am a member of a signatory community. The 2024 settlement, the past versus future split, what gets paid out and when, who decides, what happens if Ontario keeps appealing. Every time I sit down to read about it I lose the thread again.

So I started writing it out. A plain-language tracker on the OIATC site. Not legal advice, not a position piece. Just the parts I have figured out, in language a member can read on their phone.

I am almost certainly getting things wrong or missing pieces. If you spot something, please tell me and I will fix it. If it helps somebody else in your circle make sense of where things stand, share it.

## Facebook — distribution-models companion page launch (manual)

Reference URL: https://oiatc.ca/explainers/robinson-huron-treaty/distribution-models

A member wrote in this week with a sharp question I had not answered well on the explainer: how can a collective form of distribution be fair to the whole membership when about half of us live off-reserve?

Honest answer is the explainer didn't take that on directly. It said each community decides locally and left it there. That is true but it is not enough.

So I added a companion page. Three approaches communities are using right now (per-capita, collective, hybrid) laid out side by side. What each one is good at. The hard question each one has to answer to be fair, including the off-reserve access standard. Six questions any member can take to their Chief and Council.

The page does not pick a side. It treats the three models the same way, on purpose.

If you read it and think it tilts toward any one approach, or if a question you would ask isn't on there, please tell me. The page will keep updating.
