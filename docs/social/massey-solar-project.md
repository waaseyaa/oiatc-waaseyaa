# Massey Solar Project explainer

Reference URL: https://oiatc.ca/explainers/massey-solar-project

## Facebook — Russell Jones personal (manual)

There is a 141 megawatt solar farm proposed northeast of Massey, in Sables-Spanish Rivers. A council fight, petitions, a citizen committee, a 20 year IESO contract as of April, and two First Nations signed on as equity partners. Most of what you hear about it is partly true, which is exactly why it is confusing.

The reason it is confusing is that five separate decisions are happening at once, on different timelines, each decided by a different body. The township, the IESO, the province, and the Crown each control a different piece. I wrote a plain-language guide that holds them apart: what is decided, what is still ahead, and who decides what. It does not take a side on whether the project should be built.

One thing I flag, as a Sagamok member: the project sits in Sagamok territory, but Sagamok is not one of the two equity partner First Nations. The Crown still owes Sagamok a duty to consult, and that has not happened yet. That is the part worth watching.

If I got something wrong or left something out, tell me and I will fix it. Share it if it helps somebody make sense of where things stand.
